Built by a firefighter, for firefighters.

Why FireOps Calc exists

FireOps Calc was created by a firefighter to make pump calculations faster, clearer, and easier to understand on real calls and during training.

What FireOps Calc does

FireOps Calc is designed to be phone-friendly and easy to use on the rig. It focuses on three main areas:

Quick pump calculations Training & practice Reference tables

1. Calculations (main screen)

The main screen is built for fast, real-world pump work. It lets you:

  • Set up attack lines with hose size, length, and nozzle type.
  • See the calculated pump pressure (PP) based on: friction loss, nozzle pressure, elevation, appliances, and more.
  • Visually track multiple lines off the same engine.
  • Work with different hose sizes and C-values, including low-friction hose.

2. Water supply & relay (where enabled)

Depending on your version, FireOps Calc can help you estimate and track:

  • Hydrant flow using % drop.
  • Tender shuttle GPM based on round-trip times and tank sizes.
  • Relay pumping needs, including engine spacing and target intake pressure.

3. Practice mode

The practice view turns pump work into training reps. It lets you:

  • Answer pump problems and check your work against the calculator.
  • Use it as a quick drill tool for engineers, officers, or new drivers.

4. Tables view (reference charts)

The ‚ÄúTables‚Äù button gives you a quick reference for:

  • Friction loss tables for common hose sizes.
  • Nozzle pressures and typical flows.
  • Rules of thumb you can use on the fireground.

5. Department presets and custom setups (app versions)

App builds of FireOps Calc are being designed to let departments:

  • Set department-specific hose sizes, C-values, and nozzle packages.
  • Save default line setups for Line 1, Line 2, Line 3, and special lines.
  • Use those presets across calculations so the math matches your rigs.

This helps keep training, drills, and on-scene calculations consistent with your department‚Äôs actual equipment.
