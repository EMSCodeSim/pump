Scenario types to practice

Single handline

Basic attack line PDP, nozzle pressure, hose length, and elevation.

Wye and siamese

Branch lines, main supply sections, and highest-loss path decisions.

Water supply

Single supply line, two supply lines, relay pumping, and target intake pressure.

Standpipe

Building operations with interior hose, elevation, and standpipe outlet assumptions.

Master stream

Deck gun, portable monitor, appliance loss, and high-flow decisions.

Foam operations

Foam percentage, flow, and pump pressure practice.

How scenario practice should work

  1. Show a short fireground setup.
  2. Ask for the required pump pressure or supply decision.
  3. Let the student calculate the answer.
  4. Reveal the final answer with a step-by-step explanation.
  5. Offer variations that change hose length, GPM, elevation, or appliance loss.

Example training problem

Residential wye split

Engine 181 supplies a wye with 200 ft of 2½". Branch A is 150 ft of 1¾" flowing 150 GPM. Branch B is 200 ft of 1¾" flowing 185 GPM. Calculate the required pump pressure using the highest-loss branch path.

PDP = main supply FL + highest branch FL + nozzle pressure + appliance/elevation as needed
